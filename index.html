The Challenge

NASA Hunch needs an AI Medical system providing preliminary diagnostic support, triage alerts, and health guidance based on symptom input and physiological data to maintain astronaut health.

Solution

User-Friendly Design

Research

The research focuses on building a hybrid AI system that combines narrow AI (rule-based and task-specific models) with generative AI (large language models) to support astronauts in diagnosing and managing medical conditions without on-site physicians.

The system is designed to operate on a single-server partition within spacecraft, integrating data from biosensors, imaging tools, and user input to deliver real-time, explainable medical guidance.

Simulation of medical vignettes for space-specific conditions (e.g., radiation sickness, renal colic, cardiac arrhythmias) to test diagnostic accuracy.

The Basic Setup

The AI acts like a Flight Surgeon: it uses the astronaut‚Äôs profile and mission context to ask targeted questions, then delivers a diagnosis and treatment plan in a clear, structured format.

The Patient's File

The AI starts by looking at who the astronaut is, how old they are, and exactly how many days they have been in space (microgravity exposure).

Mission Context

The system tracks microgravity exposure‚Äîhow long the astronaut has been without gravity‚Äîwhich is critical for space-related medical assessment.

Listening to Problems

The astronaut tells the AI what's wrong (e.g., lightheadedness, stuffy nose, headache, stomach aches). The AI then asks smart follow-up questions‚Äîsuch as fever or chest pain‚Äîto rule out emergencies.

Making a Plan

Once it has the facts, the AI gives a diagnosis (e.g., Allergic Rhinitis, Orthostatic Intolerance, Migraine, Gastroenteritis) and recommends specific medicine with exact dosages.

Behind the Scenes

The AI knows exact doses (e.g., 10 mg Loratadine for allergies, 20 mg Midodrine for dizziness). It is programmed to tell the astronaut to report immediately if symptoms worsen or new neurological or cardiopulmonary symptoms occur. All output is structured (e.g., JSON) for clear display and logging.

Structured Output & Escalation

Diagnoses and treatment plans are output in a structured format (e.g., JSON) for logging and display. The system clearly states when to report immediately‚Äîe.g., worsening symptoms, unstable vitals, or new neurological or cardiopulmonary symptoms‚Äîand requires daily check-ins when needed.

MedOps Coach

MedOps Coach is an AI medical operations assistant designed for use during deep space missions. It runs locally on a spacecraft's onboard computer and operates in advisory-only mode, guiding astronauts through approved medical protocols.

Core Mission

  • Guide astronauts through approved medical protocols when communication with flight surgeons is delayed or unavailable
  • Never diagnose, prescribe, or invent new treatments
  • Reference Rules-Core and SOP (RAG) systems to generate safe, clear, and confirmable responses

Behavior Guidelines

  1. All medication and procedure steps must originate from validated rules-core actions or SOPs
  2. Follow structure: Action ‚Üí Reason ‚Üí Confirmation ‚Üí Next check interval
  3. Always confirm units, thresholds, and guardrails
  4. Speak in short, clear sentences suitable for voice playback
  5. Read back critical actions and ask for confirmation before proceeding
  6. Announce timers and remind users to log vitals and actions
  7. Always begin sessions with: "Advisory only. Follow flight surgeon guidance when available. Confirm all steps before action."

Tone & Style

  • Calm, professional, and concise ‚Äî like a mission specialist or flight controller
  • Use procedural phrasing with clear instructions
  • Always refer to MOP guidelines and CLS (Crew Life Support) protocols
  • When unsure: "I cannot proceed without rules-core confirmation."

Core Functions

  • Reference structured data from rules-core and rag-sop systems
  • Output JSON-safe summaries when required
  • Support voice workflows through ASR (speech-to-text) and TTS (text-to-speech) endpoints

Diagnostic Examples (Symptom ‚Üí Diagnosis & Treatment)

Based on symptom input and mission profile, the system can deliver specific diagnoses and treatment plans. Crew are instructed to report immediately if symptoms worsen, vital signs become unstable, or new neurological or cardiopulmonary symptoms occur; daily check-ins are required.

Headache ‚Üí Migraine

Treatment: Sumatriptan 50 mg orally at onset, may repeat once after 2 hours. Continue hydration and monitor symptoms closely.

Stomach aches ‚Üí Gastroenteritis

Treatment: Loperamide 4 mg initially, then 2 mg after loose stool (max 8 mg/day). Continue hydration and monitor symptoms closely.

Lightheadedness / orthostatic symptoms ‚Üí Orthostatic Intolerance

Treatment: Midodrine 20 mg orally every 8 hours. Continue hydration and monitor symptoms closely.

Sneezing, nasal congestion, itchy eyes ‚Üí Allergic Rhinitis

Treatment: Loratadine 10 mg orally once daily. Continue hydration and monitor symptoms closely.

Fever / possible infection ‚Üí Monitor & Escalate

Treatment: System assesses severity and associated symptoms; recommends hydration, monitoring, and immediate reporting if high fever, rigors, or spreading rash develop.

Chest pain / cardiac concerns ‚Üí Immediate protocol

Treatment: AI flags for immediate reporting and follows cardiac protocol; no at-home medication‚Äîescalate to flight surgeon and vital sign monitoring.
